# Persona Archetypes (19 Total)

## Part 1: Executive & Technical Leadership

### Founder-CEO

**What they care about:** Growth efficiency, CAC, revenue metrics, strategic AI investment

**Key Metrics:**
- MRR / ARR
- CAC and CAC Payback Period
- LTV:CAC ratio
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Churn Rate
- Burn rate and runway

**Pain Points:**
1. Growth slowdown:pivot from "growth at any cost" to efficiency
2. CAC efficiency crisis:ratios trending wrong direction
3. Increased competition and customer portability
4. GTM clarity:which channels actually drive pipeline

**Buying Behavior:**
- 41% have preferred vendor before formal evaluation begins
- Complete 60%+ of buying process before engaging vendor
- 13 stakeholders involved on average
- 80%+ opt for interactive demos

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your CAC is rising but conversion is flat"
- Proof points: ROI metrics, customer logos, revenue results from similar-stage companies
- CTA: "Worth a quick chat?" / specific time suggestions

**Anti-patterns:**
- Generic "fellow founder" appeals without specificity
- Long emails asking for 30+ minute meetings
- Pitching features rather than business outcomes
- Not understanding their stage (seed vs. Series B priorities differ)

---

### CTO / Founder-CTO

**What they care about:** Architecture decisions, security, developer productivity, AI adoption strategy

**Key Metrics:**
- System uptime and reliability (99.9%+ targets)
- Development velocity and deployment frequency
- Lead time for changes
- Technical debt ratio
- Security posture and compliance certifications

**Pain Points:**
1. Cybersecurity:increasing threats, robust posture needed
2. AI adoption pressure:racing to integrate but balancing practical value
3. Technical debt:quick fixes creating inefficient systems
4. 78% struggle to align tech strategy with business objectives

**Buying Behavior:**
- Business alignment focus:every tool must tie to objectives
- Pilot-first approach:less than 2% of engineering budget for POC
- ROI-driven:need clear metrics on time saved, cost efficiency
- Security-first:require attestations, certifications, SBOM visibility

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your engineers are spending X% of time on Y instead of shipping"
- Proof points: Security certifications, integration capabilities, developer productivity metrics
- CTA: "Happy to share how [similar company] reduced deploy time by X%"

**Anti-patterns:**
- Marketing fluff without technical substance
- Not understanding their tech stack
- Overselling without addressing security/compliance

---

### VP of Engineering

**What they care about:** Developer productivity, DORA metrics, scaling engineering teams

**Key Metrics:**
- DORA metrics: deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate, MTTR
- Sprint velocity and on-time delivery rates
- Team retention and developer satisfaction
- Bug rates, incident frequency, technical debt ratio

**Pain Points:**
1. Productivity crisis:developers spend only 32% of time writing code
2. Scaling complexity:at 50+ engineers, 1,225+ communication paths
3. Context switching:78% cite "too many interruptions" as primary blocker
4. Rising engineering costs:inefficient scaling costs $85,000/engineer annually

**Buying Behavior:**
- Primary lens: "Will this make my team more productive?"
- 73% expected to adopt generative AI-enabled solutions
- Integration with existing toolchain required
- Proof through pilots before company-wide rollout

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your engineers are only coding 32% of the time"
- Proof points: Developer productivity metrics, time saved per engineer
- CTA: "Worth a pilot with one team?"

---

### CIO / VP of IT

**What they care about:** AI strategy (#1 priority), security/compliance, vendor consolidation, technical debt

**Key Metrics:**
- ROI on technology investments
- System uptime/availability (99.9%+)
- Security incident metrics
- AI projects in production vs POC
- IT cost optimization

**Pain Points:**
1. AI strategy is #1 priority but 74% haven't seen tangible value
2. 42% of companies abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025
3. Cybersecurity threats and compliance requirements
4. Vendor consolidation pressure (1,000+ SaaS apps average)
5. Technical debt consuming 20-40% of IT budget

**Buying Behavior:**
- Full authority over IT infrastructure budget
- 6-12 month evaluation cycles for strategic platforms
- Security assessment mandatory (SOC 2 Type II, GDPR)
- Strong preference for vendors already in approved list
- Requires clear TCO analysis, not just license cost

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your AI initiatives are stuck in pilot purgatory:how do you scale to production?"
- Proof points: Enterprise case studies, security certifications, time-to-production metrics
- CTA: "Here's how [similar company] reduced AI deployment time from 18 months to 90 days"

**Anti-patterns:**
- Leading with features:they care about outcomes
- Ignoring security/compliance:immediate disqualification
- Hype-driven AI claims:skeptical after failures
- Point solution positioning:they want consolidation

## Part 2: Product Management

### CPO / VP of Product

**What they care about:** User engagement, feature adoption, roadmap delivery, AI integration

**Key Metrics:**
- Revenue and profitability (product revenue targets)
- Roadmap delivery (on-time, on-spec)
- Customer satisfaction: NPS, customer feedback metrics
- User engagement: product usage, feature adoption

**Pain Points:**
1. AI integration challenges:not every AI feature solves real problems
2. Competing stakeholder priorities:engineering vs. sales vs. leadership
3. Cross-team alignment and delivery
4. Becoming business strategists:boundaries disappearing

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your product decisions are based on incomplete user data"
- Proof points: User engagement improvements, feature adoption rates
- CTA: "Want to see how [similar company] increased feature adoption by X%?"

---

### Director / Head of Product

**What they care about:** Cross-functional alignment, proving ROI, time-to-insight

**Key Metrics:**
- Revenue metrics: MRR, ARR, revenue growth, CLV
- Customer metrics: NPS, CSAT, churn rate, retention
- Team metrics: velocity, product team efficiency, feature adoption

**Pain Points:**
1. Managing competing stakeholder priorities (#1 challenge in 2024)
2. Demonstrating ROI on product investments
3. Cross-functional alignment:siloed communication
4. Stakeholders proposing solutions rather than problems

**Buying Behavior:**
- Have direct budget authority for product tools
- 83% prefer self-serve during discovery and evaluation
- 57% expect positive ROI within 3 months

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: Cross-functional alignment, proving product ROI
- Proof points: Case studies showing revenue impact and time savings
- CTA: Interest-based CTAs achieve 30% response rate vs 15% for direct booking

---

### Senior PM / Group PM

**What they care about:** Feature adoption, portfolio metrics, cross-product synergies

**Buying Behavior:**
- Influence decisions heavily but may not have final budget authority
- GPMs often have budget for their product area
- Peer recommendations carry significant weight

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "How do you ensure every feature drives measurable business impact?"
- CTA: Value-first: "Happy to share how [Company] cut prioritization debates by 60%"

**Anti-patterns:**
- Treating them like individual contributors:they think strategically
- Overselling budget authority they may not have

---

### Technical / Platform PM

**What they care about:** Platform adoption, developer experience, internal customer satisfaction

**Pain Points:**
1. Quantifying infrastructure impact:link to business outcomes rarely obvious
2. Competing priorities across internal teams
3. Difficult feedback collection from internal customers
4. Technical debt management

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "How do you prove business value of platform improvements?"
- CTA: Technical deep-dive / API documentation preview

**Anti-patterns:**
- Non-technical messaging:they'll dismiss you
- Generic PM pain points without platform-specific angle

## Part 3: Customer Experience & Support

### VP of CX/CS

**What they care about:** NRR (120%+ target), churn, proving ROI to leadership

**Key Metrics:**
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR):target 120%+ for fastest-growing B2B SaaS
- Churn Rate / Logo Retention Rate
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
- NPS and CSAT
- Expansion Revenue

**Pain Points:**
1. Proving ROI:must connect programs to business outcomes
2. Cross-functional collaboration (top challenge)
3. AI adoption concerns:privacy, bias, IP infringement
4. Data fragmentation across platforms

**Buying Behavior:**
- Full budget ownership for CS technology stack
- Final say on platforms (Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango)
- Team-based decisions result in better outcomes

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your CS team is running on instinct, not data. How do you prove value to the board?"
- Proof points: Express ROI in dollars: "Delivered 300% ROI, turning $10K into $40K"
- CTA: Specific date/time CTAs double meeting bookings (15% → 37%)

---

### Director of CX/Support

**What they care about:** Breaking silos, CSAT, FRT, team management

**Key Metrics:**
- CSAT (percentage of positive 4-5 ratings)
- First Response Time (FRT)
- Average Resolution Time
- Customer Effort Score (CES)

**Pain Points:**
1. Organizational silos (73% say breaking barriers is top challenge)
2. Rising customer expectations for instant, personalized service
3. Staffing and retention:high agent turnover
4. Technology fragmentation

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Silos between support and product mean bugs take weeks to fix"
- Proof points: "Reduced FRT from 4 hours to 45 minutes"

---

### Head of Support / Support Engineering Manager

**What they care about:** Knowledge gaps, SLAs, team productivity, escalation handling

**Key Metrics:**
- First Response Time (FRT) and First Contact Resolution (FCR)
- SLA Compliance
- Team Productivity (tickets per agent)
- CSAT per agent and team-wide

**Pain Points:**
1. Knowledge/skill gaps (51% cite as top pain point)
2. Scaling challenges:lack of processes, unclear escalation paths
3. Tool sprawl creating inefficiency
4. Team burnout from high-pressure environments

**Buying Behavior:**
- Strong influencer on tool decisions; may not have direct budget
- Practical focus: "Will this make my team's life easier?"

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your support engineers are buried in repetitive tickets while complex issues pile up"
- CTA: ROI calculator or assessment tool

---

### CSM / Onboarding Manager

**What they care about:** Time-to-Value (TTV), feature adoption, renewals, avoiding burnout

**Pain Points:**
1. Burnout and stress (core issues facing CS professionals)
2. Lack of strategic seat:limited visibility and growth opportunities
3. Outdated success metrics that don't predict retention
4. Tool limitations from underfunding

**Buying Behavior:**
- Influence role:not typically decision-makers but strong influencers
- Champion potential for tools that solve daily pain points

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "You're spending hours on manual tasks instead of building customer relationships"
- CTA: Free template or checklist offer

**Anti-patterns:**
- Treating them as decision-makers (they influence, not decide)

---

### Support Team Lead

**What they care about:** Agent productivity, FCR, backlog management, team coaching

**Buying Behavior:**
- Recommender role:frontline insight but rarely holds budget
- Practical evaluation: "Will this make my team's job easier right now?"

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "You're spending time fighting fires instead of coaching your team"
- CTA: "See the 5-minute demo" / template they can use immediately

---

### Support Ops / CX Ops Leaders

**What they care about:** Tool consolidation, integration, automation ROI, data quality

**Key Metrics:**
- Cost per ticket/resolution
- Automation/deflection rate
- System uptime and integration success
- Time savings from automation

**Pain Points:**
1. Tool sprawl:81% believe consolidating to single system would improve CX
2. Fragmented data across CRMs, helpdesks, chatbots
3. Integration complexity with legacy systems
4. Automation ROI uncertainty:hard to prove time savings
5. Cross-departmental silos (73% cite as top challenge)

**Buying Behavior:**
- Often has budget for operational tools ($10K-$50K)
- Highly technical evaluation: API depth, webhooks, native integrations
- TCO focus:licenses, implementation, ongoing admin
- Proof-of-concept required in their actual environment
- Peer recommendations from ops communities carry weight

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your support data lives in 5 different systems:how do you get a single source of truth?"
- Proof points: Integration success stories, time savings ("Reduced manual routing by 15 hours/week"), TCO comparisons
- CTA: "See how [similar company] automated X and saved Y hours/week" / "Here's our API documentation"

**Anti-patterns:**
- Non-technical messaging:they'll dismiss you immediately
- Ignoring integration requirements:if you don't mention their stack, you're irrelevant
- Overpromising ease of implementation:they've been burned before
- Generic ROI claims without operational specifics

## Part 4: Developer Relations, Docs & Community

### Head of DevRel / VP DevRel

**What they care about:** Proving DevRel ROI, developer activation, content efficiency

**Key Metrics:**
- Registered developer accounts (48%) and developer satisfaction (48%)
- Primary goal: Driving awareness and adoption (66%)
- Product usage (36% track as top community metric)
- Active users (44% as primary success measure)

**Pain Points:**
1. Proving ROI:difficulty connecting to business outcomes
2. Newsletter signups falling (11.8% → 3.7%)
3. Documentation tools fragmentation
4. Balancing technical credibility vs. marketing perception

**Buying Behavior:**
- Peer-influenced:rely on vendor knowledge and peer reviews
- Will personally test tools before recommending
- Budget: Typically has authority for tools under $10-50K

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your DevRel team is spending 50% on content creation but struggling to prove ROI"
- Proof points: Impact on developer activation, time-to-first-API-call

**Anti-patterns:**
- Generic "let me pick your brain" outreach
- Treating them like marketing:they identify as technical practitioners
- Not referencing their talks, blogs, or podcasts

---

### Developer Advocate (Senior/Lead)

**What they care about:** Content creation efficiency, proving impact, conference prep

**Pain Points:**
1. Wearing many hats:too many responsibilities
2. Proving individual impact
3. Travel burnout
4. AI-generated code quality issues (45% cite "almost right" solutions)

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "You're creating amazing content but spending too much time on repetitive tasks"
- CTA: Quick async demo / Free trial with existing content

**Anti-patterns:**
- Not knowing their public work:reference their talks and blogs
- Over-promising AI capabilities:they're skeptical

---

### Junior Developer Advocate

**What they care about:** Career path, credibility building, content volume pressure

**Pain Points:**
1. Career path uncertainty
2. Credibility building with engineering peers
3. Content volume pressure
4. Tool overload
5. Imposter syndrome

**Buying Behavior:**
- Limited budget authority:recommend but rarely approve
- Trial-first:extensively test free tiers
- Peer-influenced:look for tools used by admired DevRel professionals

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "You're juggling conference prep, content creation, and community management with limited time"
- Proof points: "Used by X DevRel professionals" / Templates they can use immediately
- CTA: Free trial / Quick video demo (under 5 min)

**Anti-patterns:**
- Assuming they have budget authority
- Too formal/corporate tone
- Ignoring their growth ambitions

---

**Pain Points:**

2. Review delays:getting feedback when deadlines loom

2. Information accuracy and consistency

4. Tool limitations

## Part 5: Growth & AI

### VP/Head of Growth

**What they care about:** Lead quality, CAC, conversion rates, PLG

**Key Metrics:**
- Revenue: ARR, MRR, Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Efficiency: CAC, LTV:CAC ratio, Payback Period
- Customer: Churn Rate, Customer Lifetime Value

**Pain Points:**
1. Lead quality crisis:61% say finding high-quality leads is biggest challenge
2. Rising CAC ($500 to $10,000+ each)
3. Complex buying journeys (266 touchpoints average)
4. Self-serve revenue development

**Buying Behavior:**
- Software buying power shifted to line-of-business executives
- 60% prefer self-led purchasing with hands-on product evaluation
- 13 people involved on average in B2B purchase decisions

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your CAC has doubled in 2 years while conversion rates stayed flat"
- CTA: Question-phrased CTAs get double reply rates

**Anti-patterns:**
- Generic opening lines ("I hope you are doing well")
- Talking about ROI reduces success by 15%:show, don't tell

---

### Head of AI / AI Transformation Leader

**What they care about:** POC-to-production, data quality, governance

**Key Metrics:**
- ROI on AI spend (organizations with CAIOs report ~10% higher return)
- POC-to-production conversion rate (42% abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025)
- Business impact (80%+ haven't realized significant impact)

**Pain Points:**
1. Data quality and readiness (43% cite as top obstacle)
2. POC abandonment:Gartner predicts 50%+ gen AI pilots will be abandoned
3. Strategic alignment:74% haven't seen tangible value
4. User adoption:fear of replacement, rigid workflows

**Buying Behavior:**
- Explicit mandate to shape strategy, make investment decisions
- Decline suppliers without technical documentation, safety artifacts
- Stakeholders: CIO, CDO, CISO, Procurement, Finance, Legal

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "74% of AI projects never make it past pilot:we help you be in the 26%"
- Proof points: POC-to-production conversion, deployment time reduction

**Anti-patterns:**
- Technical jargon overload without business value
- Ignoring governance and security concerns
- Overpromising ROI:74% haven't seen value

### Content Creator (Recruiting)

**What they care about:** Budget constraints, video production cost, time pressure

**Key Metrics:**
- Time to Hire, Cost Per Hire
- Video engagement (200-300% more than text)
- Content performance, conversion to applicants
- Employer brand awareness

**Pain Points:**
1. Time and budget constraints:88% want more video but production is expensive
2. Limited budget:only 8% of HR leaders have dedicated employer branding budget
3. Content volume demands:7.5M new blog posts daily
4. Reaching young talent in competitive market
5. Speed requirements in hiring

**Buying Behavior:**
- Often limited budget:requires buy-in from multiple levels
- Decision influencers: CEO (smaller orgs), HR/Talent leads, Communications
- Evaluation: scalability, ease of use, ATS integration, time-to-value

**Messaging Angle:**
- Problem framing: "Your team creates 1 employer brand video per quarter when competitors publish weekly"
- Proof points: Time savings, cost reduction vs. agency, engagement improvements
- CTA: Personalization boosts replies ~32% in recruiting context

**Anti-patterns:**
- Ignoring budget reality (8% have dedicated budget)
- Overpromising production value
- Missing stakeholder dynamics (need multi-level buy-in)
