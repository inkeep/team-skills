## Part 1: Logo Hunting Strategy

### The Wiz Playbook Applied to Inkeep

**Wiz Pattern:** Target "thought leaders of the security world" → Land Home Depot → Fortune 500 cascade → 50% of Fortune 500 in 4 years

**Inkeep Application:**

#### Step 1: Define Marquee Logo Categories

| Category | Target Companies | Why They Matter |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **AI/ML Leaders** | Anthropic ✓, OpenAI, Cohere, Mistral | Validates AI-native approach |
| **Developer Infrastructure** | Vercel, Supabase, PlanetScale, Railway | Validates devtool focus |
| **Unicorn SaaS** | Figma, Notion, Linear, Loom | Validates enterprise-ready |
| **Enterprise Tech** | Datadog ✓, HashiCorp, Confluent, Elastic | Validates scale |

#### Step 2: Logo-First Outreach Sequence

**Week 1-2: Research & Personalization**
```
1. Identify 10 "lighthouse" accounts per quarter
2. Map org chart (VP CX, Head of Support, Head of Docs, DevRel)
3. Find specific pain signals:
   - Job postings mentioning AI/support challenges
   - G2/Gartner reviews showing competitor dissatisfaction
   - Public complaints (Discord, Twitter, community forums)
   - Documentation gaps (test their current search)
```

**Week 3-4: Multi-Stakeholder Outreach**
- Email 1 (VP CX): Business outcome framing
- LinkedIn (Head of Support): Operational pain + video
- Email 2 (Head of Docs): Content efficiency angle
- Direct mail (VP CX): Physical gift + handwritten note

**Key Insight (from Wiz):** After landing one marquee logo, reference them in EVERY subsequent outreach:
> "We work with [Anthropic/Datadog] — happy to share how they achieved [specific outcome]"

## Part 2: Signal-Based Outbound

### The Data Case

| Metric | Cold Outbound | Signal-Based (Warm) | Improvement |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Close rate | ~5% | 12.8% | 2.5x |
| Sales cycle | 81 days | 33 days | 2.5x faster |
| Reply rate | 3-5% | 8-15% | 3x |

**Source:** Warmly Research, 500+ B2B leads

### Signal Prioritization Framework

#### Tier 1 Signals (Highest Intent)

| Signal | Detection Tool | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Pricing page visit** | PostHog | Same-day AE outreach |
| **Competitor evaluation** | G2, 6sense | Comparison content + call |
| **Job posting: AI/Support** | LinkedIn, Crustdata | "Noticed you're hiring..." email |
| **Tech stack change** | BuiltWith, Crustdata | "Saw you added [tool]..." |

#### Tier 2 Signals (Moderate Intent)

| Signal | Detection Tool | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Funding round** | Crunchbase, Crustdata | Growth-focused outreach |
| **New CX/Support leader** | LinkedIn (90-day tenure) | "New broom" outreach |
| **Competitor uninstall** | BuiltWith | Displacement messaging |
| **Blog/docs content engagement** | PostHog | Content-specific follow-up |

#### Tier 3 Signals (Contextual)

| Signal | Detection Tool | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| **Expansion (new region/product)** | Press, LinkedIn | Scale pain messaging |
| **Support scaling challenges** | Glassdoor, job postings | Burnout prevention angle |
| **Community growth** | Discord member count | Community support angle |

### Implementation with Current Stack

```
Clay (enrichment) + Crustdata (signals)
  → Apollo (contact discovery)
  → Outreach.io (sequences)
  → Unify GTM (trigger automation)
  → HubSpot (CRM tracking)
```

**Workflow:**
1. **Crustdata** monitors: funding, hiring, tech stack changes
2. **Clay** enriches: contact info, technographics, company data
3. **Unify GTM** triggers: signal-based sequence enrollment
4. **Outreach.io** executes: personalized multi-channel sequences
5. **Gong** captures: conversation intelligence for iteration

5. $100K → $4.5M ARR in 12 months after pivot

**Podcast Launch: "The AI Support Show"**
- Target: VP CX, Head of Support, DevRel leaders
- Format: 30-min interviews with CX leaders
- Frequency: Weekly

Podcast listener (awareness)

**Small Events First:**

## Part 5: Sequence Optimization

### Current State Improvements

Based on B2B outbound research (Gong 85M emails, Lavender 200M emails):

#### Email Copy Optimization

| Element | Current Best Practice | Expected Impact |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Length** | Under 80 words first touch | +67% replies |
| **Reading level** | 3rd-5th grade | +67% replies |
| **CTA** | Interest-based vs meeting request | 2x positive replies |
| **Social proof** | Specific metrics, named company | +41% replies |
| **Structure** | Problem > Proof > Offer > CTA | Data-backed formula |

#### Subject Line Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| 1-4 words ("Support capacity") | Long sales-y subjects |
| Internal email look | Punctuation, emojis |
| Lowercase or title case | First name merge token |
| Question-framed (no ?) | Numbers/metrics |

#### Sequence Architecture

**Recommended Cadence:**
```
Day 1:  Email #1 — Problem + proof (new thread)
Day 3:  Email #2 — Context bump (same thread)
Day 3-5: LinkedIn connect + phone attempt
Day 7:  Email #3 — Different angle (new thread)
Day 10: Email #4 — Video/Loom (same thread)
Day 14: Email #5 — Breakup (loss aversion)
Day 21: [Optional] Final value-add
```

**Key Insights:**
- Email 2 is highest-leverage: +49% reply lift (Belkins)
- Follow-ups should be LONGER (4+ sentences = 15x more meetings)
- Video in Email 3+ can boost replies 2-3x (if personalized)
- "Never heard back" = -14% meetings. Use loss-aversion breakup instead.

#### Breakup Email Template (Loss Aversion)
```
Subject: Closing your file

[First Name],

I've reached out a few times about how [similar company]
cut tickets 48%.

I'm guessing timing isn't right — no worries, I'll close
your file for now.

If support capacity becomes a priority, I'm here.

[Signature]
```

**Why it works:** 33-76% response rate for loss-aversion breakups vs guilt-based
