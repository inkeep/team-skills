## Findings

### Finding: Inline/contextual CTAs outperform generic bottom CTAs
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Multiple B2B SaaS studies

- "Callout widgets inserted inline into blog posts are their best-performing CTAs" - StableWP
- Inline CTAs work best placed early: after introduction or first main point
- Generic "Get a Demo" CTAs at bottom don't convert blog readers
- Contextual CTAs that connect article topic to product capability convert

**Implications:** Inkeep blog posts need mid-article CTAs that bridge content topic to product value.

### Finding: Demo CTAs in blogs should feel like logical next steps
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Kalungi, SentricHR example

- "In-text CTA to schedule a demo isn't pushy or overly sales-y"
- Position as "logical next step for those serious about the solution"
- Align CTA with content above it (e.g., after discussing challenges)
- Demo conversion is "strongest conversion in B2B"

**Implications:** CTAs should be phrased as natural continuations of the content, not interruptions.

### Finding: Readers only read 60% of content - mid-placement critical
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Positional research

- "Placing CTAs within the middle of blog posts raises chances viewers will spot them before leaving"
- "Readers typically only read the first 60% of content"
- Bottom CTAs miss most readers

**Implications:** Inkeep should add CTAs at 30-50% scroll depth, not just at bottom.

### Finding: Multiple CTA options for different buyer stages
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Mouseflow, Obility

- "Provide two CTAs that offer different next steps for different buyer's journey stages"
- Example: "Preview in Figma" (hands-on) vs "Buy full version" (ready to buy)
- Low-commitment options capture earlier-stage readers

**Implications:** Blog posts could offer both "See how it works" (video/interactive) and "Book a demo" options.

### Finding: Personalized CTAs outperform default by 202%
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** HubSpot data

- Smart CTAs based on reader context dramatically outperform
- Contextual CTAs pointing to relevant content based on blog topic
- "Present the right offer to the right audience"

**Implications:** Consider dynamic CTAs based on content category (e.g., enterprise content → enterprise CTA).

## Effective CTA Types for Blogs

1. **Inline text CTA** - Simple hyperlinked text within paragraphs
2. **Callout box** - Visually distinct box with screenshot and CTA button
3. **Slide-in/sticky** - Appears as reader scrolls (less intrusive than popups)
4. **Content upgrade** - Related resource (guide, calculator) as lead magnet
5. **Exit intent** - Triggered when reader is about to leave
