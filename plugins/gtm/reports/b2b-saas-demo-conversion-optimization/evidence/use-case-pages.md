## Findings

### Finding: Use-case pages are critical conversion touchpoints
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Industry research

- "B2B SaaS product pages serve as the primary conversion touchpoint"
- "69% of purchase process happens before buyers engage sellers"
- "81% choose a preferred vendor prior to speaking with sales"
- Use-case pages must convince without sales interaction

**Implications:** Inkeep use-case pages with 50%+ bounce are failing at critical moment.

### Finding: Generic testimonials provide little value vs metric-driven stories
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Multiple studies

- "Generic testimonials provide little value"
- "Specific, metric-driven case studies provide shareable evidence"
- "Decision-makers need to justify investment with hard metrics"
- Examples: "50% ticket reduction," "18% activation increase"

**Implications:** Inkeep use-case pages need specific metrics from case studies (Fingerprint, PostHog).

### Finding: Social proof near CTAs increases conversion 84-270%
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Unbounce, industry data

- Testimonials/trust signals near CTAs dramatically improve conversion
- "Impact decreased significantly by burying social proof in footers"
- Customer logos establish category fit

**Implications:** Each use-case page needs testimonial from that segment near CTA.

### Finding: Dual CTA creates decision paralysis
**Confidence:** INFERRED
**Evidence:** Single-goal page research + Inkeep data

- Multiple CTAs reduce conversion by ~28%
- "Get Started" + "Get a Demo" side-by-side creates confusion
- Users unsure which path is right for them

**Implications:** Consider single primary CTA or clear differentiation ("Developers → Start free" / "Teams → Book demo").

### Finding: Programmatic SEO drives use-case page traffic
**Confidence:** CONFIRMED
**Evidence:** Genesys Growth

- "Programmatic SEO strategies help product pages rank for specific use cases"
- Pages targeting "[use case] + [industry]" queries capture high-intent traffic
- Comparison queries ("X vs Y for [use case]") are high-converting

**Implications:** Inkeep could create more specific use-case pages (e.g., "AI support for developer tools").

## Use-Case Page Framework

**Hero Section:**
1. Pain-point hook (specific to segment)
2. Clear value proposition for that use case
3. Single primary CTA
4. Trust signal (customer logo from that segment)

**Body:**
1. Problem → Solution narrative
2. Product screenshots showing use-case-specific features
3. Metrics from relevant case study
4. Inline CTA mid-page

**Bottom:**
1. Full testimonial quote
2. Related case study link
3. Final CTA
