# Copy Structure Patterns

Observed conventions from the Inkeep marketing site. These patterns are specific to marketing copy (website, landing pages, campaigns) — not universal brand identity. For brand vocabulary, messaging principles, value framing, and social proof phrasing that apply to ALL mediums, see `brand-guide.md`.

---

## Headline conventions

- Always declarative statements, never questions
- Often verb-first: "Ship AI Agents", "Enable your teams", "Deploy Agents anywhere"
- Three common formulas:
  1. [Verb] + [possessive] + [object] + [trust qualifier] — "AI Agents you can trust for customer operations"
  2. [Action verb] + [teams/users] + [outcome] — "Enable your teams to create and own their AI Agents"
  3. [Object] + [qualifier phrase] — "Made for customer teams", "Loved by technical teams"

---

## CTA conventions

- Primary CTA is almost always **"Get a demo"** (2-4 words, imperative, verb-first)
- Exploratory CTA is **"Learn more"** (navigation into deeper content)
- Technical CTA is **"Quick Start"** or **"Open Source"** (developers page)
- Enterprise CTA is **"Schedule a call"** (more formal)

---

## Use case page conventions

- Hero headline addresses the persona directly: "Agents your customers and support team can trust"
- How-it-works steps follow a 4-step pattern: Connect → Create → Equip → Ship/Monitor
- Footer CTA restates the core value for that persona
- Feature cards use recurring titles: "Unify your knowledge", "Agents that take action", "Close knowledge gaps"
